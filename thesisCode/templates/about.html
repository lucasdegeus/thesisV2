The internet has been one of the most growing technologies. The number of users increased from 413 million in 2000 to over 3.4 billion in 2016 [1]. With the rise of social media services and the increase in online services, people are spending more time online than before. The dominant business model for the social media services on the internet is targeted/personal advertising, which is built on user profiling [2]. Profiling requires data collection and analysis about the personal data of those users.

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) , introduced on May 25, 2018, requires all companies that provide services that handle personal data of users located in the EU to inform these users about the way their data is processed. This information is often presented in the form of a privacy policy or terms of service. Navigating through the contents of privacy policies which are normally long and difficult to read can be exhaustive. A study from Ober et al. suggest that an average adult would need 29-32 minutes to read a privacy policy for joining a social networking service [3]. The same study concludes that reading all privacy policies you encounter in a year would take 76 work days to read. It is therefore not surprising that hardly anyone reads them. Besides the length, most privacy policies lack readability which makes it hard to understand the content [4]. A survey from Deloitte with over 51000 respondents across 33 countries shows that 91% of the respondents is willing to accept legal terms and conditions for mobile apps and service providers without reading them [5]. Only 13% of the respondents never share their personal information without reading the terms and conditions.

This tool is designed to help users navigate through privacy policies faster and make it possible to read the most important information from policies.
